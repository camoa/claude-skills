# Carousels Guide

Content type guide for creating LinkedIn and Instagram carousels. Presentation Zen principles adapted for carousel format.

---

## Zen Foundation for Carousels

### Three Guiding Principles (Adapted)

**Restraint (Kanso)**
- One idea per slide - even more critical in carousels
- 10-30 words maximum per slide
- Eliminate everything that doesn't support the message
- Challenge: Can you say it with fewer words?

**Simplicity**
- Simple slides get swiped; complex slides get skipped
- Think communication, not decoration
- Visual clarity over visual complexity
- Each slide should be understood in 2 seconds

**Naturalness**
- Organic flow from slide to slide
- Narrative that feels conversational
- Story arc that pulls viewers through

### Signal-to-Noise Ratio

- Signal = your message on each slide
- Noise = everything else (clutter, decoration)
- Mobile viewing demands even higher SNR than presentations
- Viewers scroll fast - clarity is critical

**Principle**: Amplification through simplification - more powerful on mobile

### Key Adaptation: Slides Must Stand Alone

Unlike presentations where narration provides context:
- Each carousel slide must communicate independently
- No presenter to explain - the visual IS the message
- Text plays a slightly larger role (but still minimal)
- Visual hierarchy must guide reading order instantly

## Card Types

### Hook Card (Slide 1)
- **Purpose**: Stop the scroll, create curiosity
- **Content**: One compelling promise or question
- **Visual**: Bold, simple, intriguing
- **Words**: 5-15

### Content Card
- **Purpose**: Deliver value, one idea at a time
- **Content**: One key concept per card
- **Visual**: Express idea visually, text supports
- **Words**: 10-30

### Data Card
- **Purpose**: Share one insight with evidence
- **Content**: One statistic, visualized
- **Visual**: Number prominent, context clear
- **Words**: 10-20

### Story Card
- **Purpose**: Connect emotionally through narrative
- **Content**: One scene or moment
- **Visual**: Show don't tell, emotional imagery
- **Words**: 10-25

### CTA Card (Final)
- **Purpose**: Drive specific action
- **Content**: Clear direction, easy next step
- **Visual**: Simple, direct, uncluttered
- **Words**: 10-20

## Structure Patterns

### Hook → Value → Action

**Slide 1: The Hook (Capture)**
- Create immediate information gap
- One compelling promise or question
- Stop the scroll with visual impact

**Middle Slides: Value (Deliver)**
- One key idea per slide
- 10-30 words maximum
- Build logical narrative flow

**Final Slide: CTA (Convert)**
- Clear, specific action
- Tied naturally to content
- Easy next step

### Problem/Solution Pattern
1. Hook: Pain point that resonates
2. Problem depth (one aspect per slide)
3. Solution introduction
4. Key benefit (visual)
5. CTA

### Step-by-Step Guide
1. Hook: Promise of transformation
2-6. Steps (one per slide, visual + minimal text)
7. Summary insight
8. CTA

### Listicle Pattern
1. "X [Tips/Ways/Reasons]" hook
2-6. Items (one per slide, visual first)
7. Bonus insight or summary
8. CTA

## Visual Design

### White Space
- White space even more critical on mobile
- Cramped slides feel overwhelming
- Breathing room guides the eye
- Professional, premium feel

**Anti-Pattern**: Filling every pixel with content

### Visual Hierarchy
- **Size**: Headline largest, supporting text smaller
- **Weight**: Bold for key message, regular for context
- **Color**: Contrast guides attention to focal point
- **Position**: Most important element at visual center or top
- **Space**: White space around key elements

**Rule**: One clear focal point per slide

### The Big Four (CRAP)
| Principle | Carousel Application |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Contrast** | Make headline pop; guide eye to key message |
| **Repetition** | Same fonts, colors, layout patterns throughout |
| **Alignment** | Consistent element positioning across slides |
| **Proximity** | Related elements together; separate distinct concepts |

---

## Typography

### Minimum Sizes (Mobile)
- Headlines: 28-36pt
- Body text: 20-24pt

### Guidelines
- Limit to 1-2 font families
- Use weight (bold/regular) for hierarchy
- Consistent sizes across all slides
- Clear distinction between headline and body

**Rule**: If it's not easily readable on mobile, it's too small

---

## Color and Contrast

### Critical Requirement
High contrast is mandatory for mobile readability:
- Light background + dark text
- OR dark background + light text
- Test on mobile devices

### Best Practices
- 2-3 brand colors consistently
- Accent color for emphasis
- Reserve bright colors for key elements
- Test contrast ratios

## Hook Performance

Based on LinkedIn research:
1. **Personal Stories** - Highest engagement
2. **Authority/Credentials** - Builds trust
3. **Statistics** - Use sparingly
4. **Questions** - Use sparingly (can feel like bait)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Text-Heavy Slides
- Overwhelming on mobile
- Viewers swipe past
- **Solution**: Strip to minimum, express visually

### Inconsistent Design
- Unprofessional appearance
- Breaks flow
- **Solution**: Same fonts, colors, positioning

### No Clear Flow
- Slides feel disconnected
- Viewers lose interest
- **Solution**: Plan narrative arc, each slide builds

### Information Overload
- Too many ideas per slide
- Too many slides
- **Solution**: Ruthlessly cut, one idea per slide

### Weak or Missing CTA
- No clear next step
- Missed opportunity
- **Solution**: Clear, specific, single action

## Carousel Types

### Educational (7-10 slides)
1. Hook: Problem or promise
2-7. Teaching points (one per slide, visual + minimal text)
8. Key takeaway (visual summary)
9. CTA

### Storytelling (5-8 slides)
1. Hook: Intriguing opening
2-5. Story progression (scene per slide)
6. Insight/lesson
7. CTA

### Data/Stats (5-7 slides)
1. Hook: Surprising statistic
2-4. Supporting data (one insight per slide, visualized)
5. Implication/meaning
6. CTA

### Listicle (6-10 slides)
1. "[Number] [Things]" hook
2-6. Items (one per slide, visual + brief text)
7. Bonus or summary
8. CTA
